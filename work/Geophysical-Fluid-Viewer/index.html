Geophysical Fluid Viewer

2016
UI/UX paper prototyping, openFrameworks
https://github.com/Cardboard/GFAT

Description

This project was one of my primary efforts during the Summer 2016 Data Visualization Internship at the NASA Jet Propulsion Lab + Caltech. I worked with fellow interns Matt Edwards and Xiangjun Shi-Trofimov to create a visualization solution to explore some cutting edge research questions the Mark Simons lab has about interferometery based models of glacier flow. In order to develop better models for simulating glacier flow and to improve data collection techniques, researchers in the Simons lab wanted an integrated solution for visualizing information about the complex intereferometry data input into the flow model, and the output of that model. This flow model can have a multitude of components operating on vastly different time scales, so we had to create a visual grammar that would enable a user to look for potentially interesting flow characteristics or defects in the model across multiple scales. At the end of our 10 week program, our team came up with an interactive visualization prototype and an extensible design language for this challenging data space.

Process

Currently no interactive application exists for visualizing geophysical fluids with multiple geospatial and temporal layers. Matt, Xiangjun, and I decided to take a paper prototype approach to designing the interactions constituting the visualization app. We worked with Simons lab researchers to identify visual media such as 2d graphs, maps, and animations that clearly conveyed complex patterns in the interferometry data space, and leveraged the paper prototype process to figure out how elements from all these types of media could work coherently in a single visualization app.

Our final deliverable prototype was created in openFrameworks and provided a variety of interactive views. Through the process of designing our application we were able to introduce a lot of novel visual ideas to the Simons’ lab developers, and assess the viability of these ideas with the researchers.
