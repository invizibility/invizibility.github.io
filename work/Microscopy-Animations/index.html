Microscopy Animations

2011
Adobe Flash Builder, Adobe Illustrator
http://loci.wisc.edu/outreach/animations

Description

I did these informative animations as part of my independent study research at the Laboratory for Optical and Computational Imagery (LOCI) while a junior at UW-Madison. This project involved creating a series of animations in Adobe Flash Builder (rendered to .mov files) that would be used to explain certain complex optical microscopy techniques to new graduate students and researchers that begin working with the lab. Having to make creative aesthetic decisions in my work at LOCI while also having the responsibility of making it intuitively accessible and functional to scientists familiarized me with a workflow that heavily utilizes both scientific and visual design competence.

Process

The microscopy techiques of interest included: confocal scanning microscopy, multiphoton microscopy, and second harmonic generation microscopy. At the time of this project, there were no such animations or adequate visual of these processes in existence, particularly for multiphoton and second-harmonic-generation. I consulted with scientists and researchers around the lab to design the most comprehensive and intuitive visual descriptions of these techniques as an educational model. I iteratively worked on my designs until they met the rigorous requirements of the senior scientists while also being clear and informative to the newer lab technicians working on the scope. The animations are provided as a public resource on the LOCI website. I've also uploaded the animations to my personal Vimeo.
