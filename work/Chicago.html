Project Description:

I took Geography 572, "Graphic Design and Cartography" taught by Robert Roth the first semester of my senior year at UW-Madison and completed this map as one of the assignments. Students were required to create a interactive tiled map in a web browser overlaid with a "points of interest" dataset. Each type of point of interest was to be denoted with a unique, custom designed icon. I chose to visualize different types of graffiti that are present throughout the city of Chicago. In particular, I was interested in the codified distinction between "street art" and "graffiti". The beige to purple gradient heatmap was generated in QuantumGIS, and it shows the distribution of all 311 graffiti removal requests throughout the city over the past 4 years, pulled from the city database. My icon set denotes specific types of graffiti throughout the city that have been documented on Red Bull's StreetArtView or Flickr, such as gang territory graffiti or commemorative murals. Although this data was not a random sample, it got me thinking about the emergent patterns of graffiti throughout the city; wheatpasting and commissioned murals tend to be in the hipster neighborhoods while the gang territory signs are more common in less socioeconomically privileged areas.
