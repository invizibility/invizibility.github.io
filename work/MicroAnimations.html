Project Description:

I did these animations as part of my independent study research at the Laboratory for Optical and Computational Imagery (LOCI) while a junior at UW-Madison. I reached out to LOCI Director Kevin Eliceiri my sophomore year interested in working on visualization problems in the life sciences, and he brought me on as an undergraduate researcher. During my time at LOCI, Kevin had me work on several interesting projects at the intersection of computational biology and information design. Having to make creative aesthetic decisions in my work at LOCI while also having the responsibility of making it intuitively accessible and functional to scientists familiarized me with a workflow that heavily utilizes both scientific and artistic competence.

This project involved creating a series of animations in Adobe Flash Builder (rendered to .mov files) that would be used to explain certain complex optical microscopy techniques to new graduate students and researchers that begin working with the lab. These techiques included: confocal scanning microscopy, multiphoton microscopy, and second harmonic generation microscopy. At the time of this project, there were no such animations or adequate visual of these processes in existence, particularly for multiphoton and second-harmonic-generation. I consulted with scientists and researchers around the lab to design the most comprehensive and intuitive visual descriptions of these techniques as an educational model. The animations are provided as a public resource on the LOCI website.
