Structure and Function: An Introduction

2016-09-28

As mentioned in my introductory post, my culminatory work of this thesis project will be a portfolio of 3 design solutions to unique visualization challenges in genomics. One of these design projects is a collaboration with the Guttman Laboratory for lncRNA Biology, in which I will explore the creation of workflows for researchers interested in relationships between the physical structure of a genome inside a cell, and its functionality.

This project is an extension of some of the work I did this past summer as an intern in the JPL/Caltech Data Visualization Program. Over the summer, I worked with other interns and researchers at the Guttman Lab to understand the challenges involved in studying relationships between genomic structure and function. We learned that there are valuable insights to be uncovered through the analysis of DNA conformation data, but this analysis requires the navigation of very opaque and complicated datasets.

The 3 dimensional shape of DNA inside a cell consists of entanglements that occur across several orders of magnitude. The following graphic from Ea et. al 2015 shows the way 2 meters of DNA sequence are packaged into 5 microns of spherically confined space in the center of the nucleus.

Understanding the unique circumstances under which a sequence will fold in different ways is critical to understanding how cells can exhibit a diverse array of behaviors driven by the same sequence, and how defects in genome structure can cause cancer and many other disease. The process of coming to such an understanding currently is a complex multistage process. Researchers must dive into massive conformation capture datasets to observe patterns of topological association between certain segments of DNA sequence.
